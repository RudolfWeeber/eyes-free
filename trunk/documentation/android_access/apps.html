There are over 70,000 apps on the Android market, and many of these are quite accessible! This is just a tiny list of some of the accessible apps and known-inaccessible apps to save you time. Please send information about more apps!

Accessible apps

  • AContacts - a contacts manager.
  • Advanced Task Manager - force quit or uninstall apps.
  • Amazon.com - search for products and purchase them from your Android device. The app is accessible but unfortunately the detailed description for each product is an inaccessible Web View. It's easier if you set up an Amazon.com account from your desktop computer first.
  • Amazon MP3 - search for music, preview, and purchase music on your phone. It's easier if you set up an Amazon.com account from your desktop computer first.
  • Calendar - the agenda view is somewhat accessible, and you can get notifications on your phone. The weekly and monthly views are not accessible. Adding or editing calendar entries on your phone is possible but a little tricky. It works great to create calendar entries from your desktop computer and then sync it to your device using Google Calendar.
  • Camera Magic - good accessible basic camera app.
  • Color Namer
  • Contacts - the built-in contacts app is fully accessible.
  • Eyes-Free Shell - an eyes-free touch-screen home screen replacement.
  • Facebook - the official Facebook app. If the home screen doesn't read, just click once to go to your news feed.
  • File Manager by Apollo software.
  • Foursquare - a social app to tell your friends where you're hanging out.
  • Good Morning - another alarm clock app, uses the trackball for snoozing and turning off the alarm.
  • Google Voice - a great solution for voicemail.
  • Grandfather Clock - chimes on the hour
  • GStrings - an instrument tuner
  • Handcent SMS - an alternative SMS app with lots of features.
  • IDEAL Accessible K9 E-Mail - an accessible version of the K9 E-Mail program, which was itself based on the built-in Android E-Mail program. It's a complete and full-featured mail program.
  • IMDB - mobile interface to the Internet Movie Database
  • Messaging - the built-in SMS app.
  • Maps - including getting driving, walking, or public transportation directions.
  • Market - see Android Market for some tips on how to use the Market.
  • mem - a memory game
  • MLB At Bat 2010 - for major league baseball fans.
  • Music - the built-in music app is accessible.
  • NoLock - gives you an option to turn on or off the slide-to-unlock screen.
  • NPR News - listen to radio streams from National Public Radio in the USA.
  • OI Notepad - to take notes and organize them.
  • Onion News Now - a parody of a news site.
  • Pianoman - a touch-screen piano.
  • Pet Android Widget - can answer simple questions like "google stock" or "time new york" if you double-tap and speak out loud.
  • Places - comes with Google Maps, lets you explore nearby businesses and points of interest.
  • Phone - the built-in phone is fully accessible.
  • Randroid: Random xkcd - a nerdy web comic.
  • Ringdroid - create ringtones from your music.
  • Rock Lock - an eyes-free touch-screen music player, can optionally be used as a lock screen.
  • Scanner Radio - Listen to police, fire scanners.
  • Stitcher - subscribe and listen to podcasts.
  • Swift - a Twitter client.
  • Talking Compass - tells you what direction you're facing if your device has an internal compass.
  • Talking Dialer - an accessible touch-screen dialer, so you don't have to open your keyboard.
  • Task Manager by Futon Redemption.
  • The vOICe for Android - camera, color identifier, and augmented reality.
  • The Weather Channel - a full-featured weather app.
  • Urban Dictionary - look up the definition of slang terms and phrases.
  • Ustream - watch streaming video.
  • Voice - lets you access Google Voice for voicemail or to use Google Voice for all phone calls. Mostly accessible but with some issues. You need to sign up for Google Voice from your desktop computer first.
  • Where - gives local places, weather, and news.
  • YouTube - the built-in YouTube app is accessible. Press the spacebar to pause and resume when you get to the main video screen.

Inaccessible apps

  • Browser - the built-in browser isn't accessible - but see the web access plug-in from the IDEAL Group for a workaround.
  • Camera - try Camera Magic instead
  • Gmail - try IDEAL Accessible K9 E-mail instead.
  • Email - try IDEAL Accessible K9 E-mail instead.
  • Twitter - it's partly usable, but has some inaccessible parts. Try Swift instead.
  • News and Weather.
