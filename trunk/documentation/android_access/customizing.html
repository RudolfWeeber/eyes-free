One of the best things about Android is that almost everything is customizable. This is something that all users can enjoy, but it's particularly helpful for accessibility, because just about anything on the phone that's inaccessible or not fully accessible can be replaced by another app that's more accessible.

Voices

Android comes preinstalled with a voice based on the SVOX Pico engine, but you are not limited to using that voice. There are currently three other choices on the market.
  • eSpeak is a free voice. It sounds a little unusual, but is free, fast, and supports dozens of languages.
  • Loquendo TTS Susan is a commercial voice. While it's not free, it's not very expensive and the quality is very good. It currently only support US English.
  • SVOX Classic is a set of commercial voices. Like the Loquendo voice, while each voice is not free, they are also not very expensive and the quality is very good. It currently supports several languages.

    In addition, IDEAL has an accessible voices sampler application called "Best Voices for Android" that lets you listen to previews of these SVOX Classic voices.
Installing a voice requires a couple of extra steps:
  1. Open the app to download any voice data and finish the installation.
  2. Enable the voice in the Android Settings. In Android 2.1 or earlier, this setting is found under the Text-to-speech menu. In Android 2.2 it is found under Voice input/output.

Screen lock

As described in Basics, by default you need to perform a swipe gesture to unlock the screen. You can install applications that eliminate the need for the gesture, or replace it with a separate lock screen which requires a password. A couple of apps to get your started:
  • No Lock is a simple app which allows you to enable or disable the screen lock via a checkbox.
  • Rock Lock is a screen that lets you listen to music while the phone is locked. It also makes a great music player.
Search the Market to find many other lock screen apps.

Home screen

The Eyes-free shell is a home screen designed to be used without looking at the screen. It uses an innovative touch-screen input method that lets you access a lot of functionality without being able to see the screen, and without needing to open the keyboard.

To use, press and hold your finger near the center of the screen. Pretend that you are in the center of a 3 x 3 grid of squares. Drag your finger to each of the 9 cells in the square to hear a description of the cell. Release your finger while over a cell to select that shortcut.

The Eyes-Free Shell comes with a separate app, Shortcuts Manager for Eyes-Free Shell, that allows you to customize the shortcuts

For more information on how to use the Eyes-free shell and other Eyes-free Android apps, see the Eyes-free YouTube videos.

Email

The email apps that come preinstalled with Android are not yet accessible. You can download an accessible version of the K9 mail program, IDEAL Accessible K9 mail from the Market. For more information on this app, see the IDEAL website.

Web

The default Android web browser is not yet accessible. The IDEAL Web Access Plug-In provides an accessible alternative. See the news release from the IDEAL Group for additional details.
