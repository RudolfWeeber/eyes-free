TalkBack is just one of several Accessibility Services that you can use on your Android device. Here are some more details about all of them:

  • KickBack is one of the official accessibility services provided by Google. It gives you haptic feedback by vibrating the device briefly when you perform an action on the phone. It's especially useful when using the touch screen, for example if you need to find the slide-to-unlock handle, but it's also useful when navigating your device using a directional controller, as you know when your movement has resulted in an action. For example when navigating through a menu, you'll feel a bump every time you hit a new menu item, but when you get to the end, you'll stop getting the feedback.
  • SoundBack is one of the official accessibility services provided by Google. It plays a short sound when you perform an action on the phone, such as moving to a different control using the directional controller, or clicking on a control that performs an action.
  • TalkBack is one of the official accessibility services provided by Google. It uses synthesized speech to describe both actions, such as moving to a new control with the directional pad or clicking on a control, and events such as a notification or incoming call.
  • Spiel is a third-party accessibility service written to be an alternative to TalkBack. It also uses synthesized speech to describe actions and events, but it has its own rules for speaking that may differ from TalkBack's. Spiel isn't currently available on the Android market, but you can download it from http://spielproject.info/.

The different accessibility services are designed to complement one another. While you should normally only one talking accessibility service at once, services that provide alternate feedback can be used simultaneously for improved efficiency. While there's a slight delay before speech synthesis can talk after you perform an action, KickBack and SoundBack are much more immediate, making the user interface faster to navigate when you're just trying to find the end of a list, for example.
